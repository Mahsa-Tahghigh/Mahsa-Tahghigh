I am Mahsa Tahghigh, a Ph.D. Candidate in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at Howard University, Washington, D.C., under the supervision of Dr. Hassan Salmani. My research expertise lies in Hardware Security, specifically focused on hardware Trojan detection in manufactured chips using side-channel analysis (EM/power signals) and unsupervised machine learning.I have a background in VLSI design and EDA tools (Cadence Virtuoso, Vivado). Contributed to a chip tapeout project in collaboration with Apple and Howard University.

I am also the recipient of the Academic Excellence Award from the DAC Young Fellow 2026 program and the Student Achievement in Microelectronics Award (April 2025), recognized by the HBCU CHIPS Network. This honor was awarded for outstanding research in microelectronics, sponsored by Micron, GlobalFoundries, and Cadence.
